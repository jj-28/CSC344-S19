Which License Type is the best for me?

MP3 Lease

MP3 leases are perfect if you're just starting out on your journey as a recording artist. The lease is relatively inexpensive, and is of decent quality. However, MP3 is a lossy format. It sacrifices size for quality, and does not contain as much information as a format such as WAV. This information is critical when it comes to mixing.

WAV Lease

WAV files are more for artists that are a bit more serious with their sound. It allows the vocal to "sit" on the beat better A WAV file is the uncompressed stereo mix of the beat. It does not have any compression, and is usually much larger than an MP3 file.

WAV Trackout Lease

WAV Trackouts gives the artist full control over the sound and arrangement of an instrumental. Each individual sound (ie: Snare, Piano, Bell, etc/) in the instrumental is exported to a Stereo WAV This allows you to change the arrangement of a song with ease. You can shorten or lengthen a hook section, or take it out completely.

Unlimited Use

The unlimited lease is similar to the WAV Track out lease, but the artist has unlimited rights to the beat. This option is more for professional artists that don't want their rights to expire.This means that an artist can do the following:

  • Sell an unlimited amount of copies of the track
  • No view or stream cap on DSPs
  • Lease and rights do not expire
